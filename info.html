We understand the times are difficult for money, so we've come up with a few tips to help reduce your carbon footprint.

1. Try to avoid plastic, or recycle. We understand that this is a pretty hefty task nowadays, but the payoff is well worth it! micro and macroplastics are often harmful to wildlife, as many know, however what you may not know is that much more things are recycleable than you'd think. Check with your local council for the full scoop

2. Use more blankets and clothes instead of heating. A lot of people have become dependent on heating in the colder months, which works for some, however for the more carbon-conscious it's more beneficial to keep windows closed, and stock up on thick, warm clothes and blankets to stay warm Overall, we find it much more cozy anyway!

3. Try to walk, or cycle to more places. It's well-known now that combustion-engine cars are harmful to the environment, but the best option for those who don't have one is to walk, or use a bicycle for shorter commutes. They're cheap, carbon-efficient, and are scientifically proven to be beneficial to your mental wellbeing. Two birds with one stone!
